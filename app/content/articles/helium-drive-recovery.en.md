## What is a helium drive?

A helium drive is a mechanical hard drive filled with helium instead of air. Helium is about 1/7 the density of air, reducing drag so manufacturers can use thinner platters and fly heads closer to the surface, enabling **10 TB and larger capacities**.

Representative models: WD Ultrastar He series, Seagate Exos X series.

## Recovery challenges

### 1. Welded lid

Traditional drives use screws for the top cover. Helium drives **weld the lid shut** to maintain the helium seal. Opening the drive requires special tools and processes to cut the seal; any mistake destroys the internal environment and the platters.

### 2. More precise heads

Helium drives have more heads and higher density, making head replacement far harder than on air-filled drives. Donor matching is also stricter.

### 3. More complex firmware

Large-capacity drives have larger and more complex SA modules, raising the bar for firmware repair.

## Field recommendations

- **Check firmware first**: if the drive is not detected, do not assume it needs opening — firmware issues are common.
- **Do not open it yourself**: helium-drive opening is extremely risky without proper equipment and usually destroys the drive.
- **Back up early**: helium drives hold a lot of data; when they fail, the loss can be huge. Keep multiple backups of important data.
